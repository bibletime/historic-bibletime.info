Galerie

Sie können hier einige schöne Bildschirmfotos von BibleTime finden, die jeweils ein oder zwei Textmodule zeigen. Sie demonstrieren die möchtigen Unicode-Funktion von Trolltechs QT 3. Einige der Bildschirmfotos sind recht beeindruckend.

Most of the screenshots were created using the superb Code2000 Unicode font. Click on the preview images to see a large and detailed version of each screenshot.

BHS - Darstellung von Hebräisch.  The BHS (short for Biblia Hebraica Stuttgartensia), the best available text of the Hebrew Old Testament. As you can see, BibleTime renders the text correctly from right to left. Those of you who can read Hebrew will notice that the Vowel Points and the Cantillation Marks (you can switch both of them on and off) are displayed correctly. Large screenshot

Peshitta - frühe syrische Übersetzung.  The Peshitta contains an early Syrian text, running from right to left. QT renders this text very nice with Code2000, which is one of the very few fonts that support syrian characters. Large screenshot

Die Arabische Bibel.  AraSVD: The Arabic Bible. Very nice example for right-to-left rendering of a complex script - Arabic is probably one of the most complicated scripts of the world. This screenshot was taken after linebreaks were switched off, providing a more readable text view. Large screenshot

Darstellung von Chinesisch.  ChiGU: Chinese Bible text. Since QT 3 BibleTime can correctly break the paragraphs (as you may know, there are no spaces between words). Large screenshot

Darstellung von Griechisch mit Strong-Nummern und morphologischen Markern.  IGNT: The Interlinear Greek New Testament. The green numbers in "< >" brackets are Strongnumbers, whereas the numbers in "( )" brackets are Morphologic Tags. Both of them can displayed in a tooltip using a predefined Strong's and Morphology dictionary, as you can see in the example. Large screenshot

Koreanisch und Thai.  Kor and ThaiKJV. Korean and Thai are very good Unicode examples covered by an amount of Unicode scripts already. They are displayed in a parallel view in one window. The first line of Thai text is of a little smaller pointsize. It is a section heading, and those can be turned on and off using the first toolbutton in the display window. Large screenshot

Bulgarisch und Fußnoten.  Bulgarian: The Bible in Bulgarian language. Bulgarian uses the Kyrillic script, which is also used in other languages like Russian. Notice the marked text - it is a "footnote". The popup menu shows how those can be turned on and off. Large screenshot

Vietnamesisch und Rumänisch.  Viet and RomCor. East Europe meets East Asia! Vietnamese and Romanian are not geographically linked, though they look somehow similar in this screenshot. Large screenshot

Esperanto ohne Versnummern und Zeilenumbrüchen.  YES, there is a Bible for those of you who prefer to speak Esperanto. This is not really a Unicode example, since the displayed characters come from the iso 8859-1 charset. But this shows how text looks like after switching off versenumbers and linebreaks. Large screenshot
